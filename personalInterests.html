    Personal interests

    Beyond my academic pursuits, I have a keen interest in photography and sports, areas that I believe are crucial for personal well-being and human growth. Tennis, in particular, has been a significant part of my life for about fourteen years. Practicing this sport has not only enriched my social and competitive experience but has also contributed to developing my coordination, concentration, and resilience, qualities that are transferable to various contexts outside the gym. Skiing is another important aspect of my extracurricular life. Skiing offers the opportunity to immerse oneself in nature, enjoy the beauty of snow-covered landscapes, and experience intense emotions related to speed and adventure. This hobby allows me to relax and find balance between academic commitments and daily life, while also providing occasions to test physical and mental limits. Chess and checkers represent yet another core area of interest. Both require deep strategic thought and a great deal of patience, qualities that I believe are essential not only in everyday life but also in the field of cybersecurity. The ability to plan strategic moves, anticipate possible scenarios, and adapt quickly to unexpected changes are skills that I find applicable when tackling complex problems in my role as a data scientist. In conclusion, alongside my academic training, my passions for photography, sports, and strategic games constitute fundamental elements of my personality and approach to life. These activities not only allow me to unwind and have fun but also help me develop personal qualities and cross-functional skills that are precious in every aspect of my existence.
